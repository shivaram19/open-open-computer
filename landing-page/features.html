Research that sticks

Upload papers, articles, books, PDFs. Ask questions across all of them at once. Sukha connects ideas you'd never see alone — and remembers them permanently.

Unlike ChatGPT, where you paste text and hope it doesn't forget the first paragraph, Sukha stores everything in a knowledge graph. Cross-references persist. Connections deepen over time.

"Find every paper that connects sleep and creativity, then summarize the common thread across all of them."
😫 The old way

Open 20 tabs. Read half of each. Forget why you opened them. Start a Google Doc. Lose the doc. Repeat next week.

✓ The Sukha way

Dump everything in. Ask questions in plain English. Get answers that cite your sources. Come back in a year and pick up exactly where you left off.

😫 The old way

Learn Premiere Pro. Watch 40 tutorials. Give up. Hire someone on Fiverr. Wait a week. Pay $200. It's not what you wanted.

✓ The Sukha way

Describe the scene. Sukha scripts, storyboards, and generates. You watch it render. Adjust. Regenerate. Done in an afternoon.

Videos from a sentence

Describe a scene or story in plain English. Sukha plans, scripts, and generates video. No editing software. No learning curve.

The video generation agent collaborates with the memory agent to pull your brand colors, past preferences, and uploaded reference footage. It's not generic. It's yours.

"A 30-second bakery ad: warm bread, morning light, a happy customer taking a bite. Use my brand colors from last month's campaign."

A co-author who knows you

Blogs, essays, fiction, reports. Sukha learns your voice, remembers your sources, cites them properly, and never says "as an AI language model."

The writing agent consults your memory before drafting. It knows you prefer short paragraphs. It knows you hate the word "leverage." It knows your audience is skeptical engineers.

"Write a blog about urban gardening. Use the studies I shared last month. Match my casual tone. Avoid the words 'leverage' and 'synergy.'"
😫 The old way

Stare at a blank page. Write a paragraph. Delete it. Open ChatGPT. Get something generic. Rewrite the whole thing anyway.

✓ The Sukha way

Ask for a draft. It pulls your past writing style. It finds relevant research. It writes in your voice. You edit. It learns from your edits.

😫 The old way

Save to Notes. Forget about it. Save to Notion. Lose it in folders. Bookmark a page. Never look at it. Your knowledge is scattered and dead.

✓ The Sukha way

Everything lives in one connected map. Search by meaning. "That thing about sleep" finds the paper, the note, the conversation.

A second brain that never forgets

Every note, bookmark, conversation, and idea lives in one connected knowledge graph. Search by meaning, not just keywords. Ask about it in ten years.

The memory agent doesn't just store text. It understands relationships. It knows that "Stoicism" connects to "Marcus Aurelius" connects to "morning routine."

"What was that book about Stoicism I mentioned in March? Show me my notes, and connect it to anything else I've saved about habits."
🎙️

Talk like a human

Speak naturally. Sukha understands emotion, context, and history. Not just the words — what you actually mean.

The voice agent picks up on tone. It knows when you're frustrated vs. curious. It adjusts its responses. It remembers that you prefer examples over definitions.

"I'm overwhelmed with my thesis. Help me break this into weekly chunks I can actually do — nothing too ambitious, I have a day job."
😫 The old way

Type everything. Edit three times to get the tone right. The AI doesn't get sarcasm. It doesn't know you're exhausted. It gives you a 20-step plan.

✓ The Sukha way

Talk naturally. It reads your tone. It remembers you have a day job. It gives you a 3-step plan. It checks in next week.

😫 The old way

Watch a tutorial. Don't understand. Watch another. Still don't understand. Give up. The tutor doesn't know what confused you. They just keep going.

✓ The Sukha way

Ask a question. Get an answer. Say "I didn't get that part." It remembers. It tries a different explanation. It adapts to how you think.

🧩

Learn at your own pace

Sukha adapts to how you think. Remembers what confused you. Explains differently next time. Patient. Permanent. Never judges.

The learning agent tracks your understanding. It knows you grasped wave-particle duality but got stuck on superposition. It comes back to superposition with a different angle. It's the tutor you wish you'd had.

"Explain quantum computing like I'm 12. Use that analogy I liked before — the one with the library books."
