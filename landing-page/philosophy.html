Built to last,
not to break.

Most software is duct tape and hope. When something breaks, everything breaks. We built Sukha using five principles that keep it stable, safe, and easy to improve — even years from now.

Five principles, zero jargon

🎯

One job per specialist

What it means: Every part of Sukha does exactly one thing. The memory agent only stores and retrieves. The writing agent only writes. The safety agent only checks for problems. They don't overlap. They don't interfere.

Why you care: If the video generator breaks, your memory still works. If the voice engine gets an update, your research doesn't glitch. Like a hospital where each doctor has one specialty instead of one person trying to be every specialist.

🏗️

New features don't break old ones

What it means: We design each part so new capabilities slot in without touching what already works. The core stays stable. Extensions plug in around it. Nothing gets rewritten just to add something new.

Why you care: When we add video generation next year, your two years of stored memories don't get corrupted. When we add a new AI model, your old workflows keep working. Like adding a new room to your house without knocking down walls.

🔌

Parts swap without breaking

What it means: Every component plugs into a standard socket. The memory system doesn't care which AI model you use. The voice engine doesn't care which language you speak. They're interchangeable by design.

Why you care: Don't like the default AI model? Swap it. Want a different memory system? Swap it. You're never locked in. No vendor owns your setup. Like changing the battery in your phone — standard socket, any brand.

🎛️

You only see what matters to you

What it means: The writer's interface shows writing tools. The researcher's interface shows research tools. The developer's interface shows code tools. Each person gets exactly what they need — nothing more, nothing less.

Why you care: No overwhelming dashboards. No hunting through menus. No "I didn't know that feature existed." When you open Sukha to write, you see writing. When you open it to research, you see research. Like walking into a kitchen and seeing kitchen things.

🧭

Big decisions don't depend on tiny details

What it means: The way Sukha behaves depends on clear contracts and agreements between parts — not on the specific code inside each part. We can upgrade any piece without rewriting everything else.

Why you care: When a better AI model comes out next month, we swap it in. Your memories, preferences, and workflows stay exactly the same. Stable today. Stable tomorrow. Like replacing the engine in your car without redesigning the steering wheel.
