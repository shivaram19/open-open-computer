What sucks about AI today?

If you've used ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini, you know these headaches. We built Sukha to fix each one.

😰

It forgets everything overnight

You pour your heart out. The next day, it's a stranger. Every conversation starts from zero.

Sukha builds a permanent memory of everything you share. Ask about something from two years ago. It remembers.
🤥

It sounds confident but makes things up

One fake fact ruins a report, a decision, or your trust. There's no warning label.

Multiple AI specialists independently check every answer. If they disagree, you see the disagreement. No blind trust.
👁️

Your private chats train someone else's product

Every thought, fear, and idea you share becomes data for a company you don't know.

Runs entirely on your computer. Your data never leaves your house. Open source. You can read every line of code.
🤯

You need five different apps for one task

One app for chat, another for images, another for voice. They don't talk. Subscriptions pile up.

One system that sees, hears, reads, remembers, and creates. Like hiring a full team that shares one brain.
💸

Another $20 a month for features you barely use

The good stuff is always behind a paywall. Stop paying, lose everything you built.

Completely free. No subscriptions. No usage limits. Your data lives in files on your computer that you own forever.
🔧

When it breaks, you're on your own

Closed systems die quietly. One API change and your workflow collapses with no warning.

Built to heal itself. If one part breaks, the rest detect it, isolate it, and keep working. Like a body fighting a cold.
